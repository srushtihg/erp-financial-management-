ERP System:
Financial Management Subsystem
1 Introduction
The Financial Management Subsystem is a core module of the Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system, responsible for managing financial transactions, accounting processes, budgeting, forecasting, taxation, and financial reporting within the organization. It integrates multiple financial functions to ensure efficient handling of financial data.
This subsystem ensures accurate financial tracking, compliance with accounting standards, and provides real-time financial insights to support informed decision-making and effective financial control.

Budgeting

2. Inputs and Outputs of Each Component
2.1 Accounts Payable
Inputs: Vendor invoice details, Purchase order data, Payment terms, Tax information. Outputs: Payment confirmation records, Expense entries, Updated payable status, Journal entries posted to General Ledger.
2.2 Accounts Receivable
Inputs: Customer invoice details, Sales transaction data, Payment receipts. Outputs: Updated customer account balances, Revenue entries, Cash inflow records, Journal entries posted to General Ledger.
2.3 General Ledger
Inputs: Journal entries from Accounts Payable and Accounts Receivable, Asset depreciation entries, Revenue recognition entries. Outputs: Trial balance, Ledger reports, Financial statements, Data for Financial Reporting.
2.4 Cash Management
Inputs: Bank transaction records, Payment records, Receipt records. Outputs: Updated cash balance, Bank reconciliation reports, Cash flow data.
2.5 Budgeting
Inputs: Department budget requests, Historical financial data, Revenue targets. Outputs: Approved budgets, Budget allocation reports, Budget variance data.
2.6 Forecasting
Inputs: Historical financial data, Sales trends, Budget information. Outputs: Revenue forecasts, Expense forecasts, Profit projections.
2.7 Revenue Recognition
Inputs: Sales contracts, Delivery confirmations, Customer invoices. Outputs:
Recognized revenue entries, Compliance reports, Journal entries to General Ledger.
2.8 Asset Management
Inputs: Asset purchase details, Asset value, Depreciation method. Outputs:
Depreciation schedule, Asset valuation report, Journal entries to General Ledger.
2.9 Financial Reporting
Inputs: General Ledger data, Budget data, Forecast data, Cash management data. Outputs:
Balance Sheet, Profit and Loss Statement, Cash Flow Statement, Financial summary reports.
2.10 Tax Management
Inputs: Financial transaction data, tax rules, regulatory policies.
Outputs: Tax calculations, tax reports, compliance data, entries to General Ledger.
2.11 Data & Audit
Inputs: Transaction logs from Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, and General Ledger.         Outputs: Audit trails, stored financial data, logs for compliance and tracking.
3. Component Diagram
The component diagram represents the modular architecture of the Financial Management Subsystem, organized into logical packages such as transaction processing, core accounting, financial planning, cash operations, reporting, and data auditing. This structured organization ensures clear separation of responsibilities and improves maintainability and scalability of the system.
Components such as Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Revenue Recognition, and Asset
Management are responsible for generating financial transactions and posting them to the General Ledger. The General Ledger acts as the central component of the subsystem, consolidating all financial data and maintaining a unified and consistent financial record.
Supporting components including Cash Management, Budgeting, Forecasting, and Tax
Management provide additional financial data and interact with the General Ledger. The
Financial Reporting component generates key financial reports such as balance sheets and profit & loss statements, while the Data & Audit component maintains logs to ensure traceability, compliance, and data integrity across the subsystem.
